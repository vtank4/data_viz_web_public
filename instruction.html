Instruction

Context of Visualizations

Alcohol consumption is a critical public health issue, significantly impacting global mortality and health conditions such as liver disease, cardiovascular diseases, and various cancers. To reduce alcohol-related harm and improve health outcomes, it is essential for countries to understand and monitor alcohol consumption patterns and their effects.

Analyzing alcohol consumption trends and mortality rates is crucial for developing effective public health policies and interventions. These visualizations highlight the correlation between alcohol consumption and total deaths, providing valuable insights for public health officials, researchers, policymakers, and the general public.

An in-depth understanding of regional and temporal trends in alcohol consumption helps identify significant changes and global patterns. This information supports the creation of targeted health campaigns and policies, ensuring informed decision-making. Additionally, by recognizing the impact of public policies and health campaigns on alcohol use, these visualizations contribute to the development of strategies aimed at reducing alcohol-related health risks and improving overall public health.

Introduction for Scatter Plot (Visualization 1)

This visualization shows the relationship between the total number of deaths and alcohol consumption per capita in various regions, with the radius of the circle representing the number of total deaths. Use the filters to view data for specific regions or time periods:

  • Play Button : Animates (Play) the scatter plot over the years.
  • Pause Button : Pauses the animation.
  • Reset Button : Resets the visualization to the initial state.
  • Slider: Manually select a year to view data.
  • Region Filter: Select a region to filter the data shown in the scatter plot. The color for each region is based on the Olympic flag for better observation.

When you hover over a circle in the scatter plot, information about the country, region, total deaths, and alcohol consumption for that year will be displayed. Other circles will blur to provide a better view.
You can choose more than one region filter.

Introduction for Line Chart (Visualization 2)

This visualization shows the trend of alcohol consumption in OECD countries from 2010 to 2022. Use the filters to view data for specific countries or time periods:

  • Country Select: Choose one or multiple countries to view their data. Hold the CTRL key to select multiple countries. Countries in the same regions will have the same color based on Olympic flag .
  • Start Year: Input the starting year for the data range.
  • End Year: Input the ending year for the data range.

When you hover over the dots on the line of each country, data about alcohol consumption for that year will appear.
